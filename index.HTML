Case Study: Musang King (D197) Growth Dynamics

Understanding the Sigmoidal Derivative in Fruit Development

In Sustainable Food Management (SFM), anticipating harvest dates and optimizing fertilization schedules is paramount to minimizing food waste and maximizing smallholder yield stability. The growth of a Musang King durian from anthesis (flowering) to natural maturity (abscission) follows a biological Sigmoid (Logistic) Curve.

By examining the derivative \(f'(x)\) (the rate of change of fruit mass), agro-biologists can pinpoint the exact day of maximum cell expansion. Applying specialized potassium-rich fertilizers at this precise inflection point boosts yield size, ensuring national food quality standard compliance.

Mathematical Equations

The Logistic Growth Model

Fruit Mass Function \(f(x)\)
\(f(x) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)}}\)
Growth Rate Derivative \(f'(x)\)
\(f'(x) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(x - x_0)}}{(1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)})^2}\)
Where \(L\) is carrying capacity (max mass), \(k\) is growth rate coefficient, and \(x_0\) is mid-point of growth (inflection point).

Why Growth Derivation Matters?

If extreme El Niño weather reduces local rainfall, transpiration is affected. This drops the carrying capacity \(L\) and shifts the inflection day \(x_0\). Real-time derivative models alert agronomists to alter irrigation intervals, stabilizing yields to shield domestic supply chains from high-inflation shocks.
